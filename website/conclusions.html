Conclusions (Early)

This section will expand in the final submission with non-technical conclusions and supporting images.

Early exploration suggests that popularity and ratings represent different types of success. Some movies achieve high visibility and engagement while not necessarily achieving the highest ratings. In contrast, other movies earn strong ratings but may not appear among the most visible titles.

The distributions of revenue and budget appear highly skewed, meaning a small number of movies dominate financial signals. This suggests that future analyses should use careful comparisons across groups and avoid relying only on simple averages. As later modules add additional modeling methods, these patterns will be explored more thoroughly to support clearer takeaways about what factors tend to align with different success outcomes.
