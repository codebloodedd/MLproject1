What makes a movie “successful”?

This project explores how movie attributes (genre, runtime, budget, revenue, release timing, and audience engagement) relate to two common notions of success: popularity and ratings.

Topic Background

Movies are more than entertainment; they are cultural products influenced by creative decisions, marketing, distribution strategy, and audience preferences. A film’s “success” can be measured in multiple ways. Some films become widely known and discussed (high popularity), while others earn strong audience approval (high ratings), even if they are less visible in mainstream channels. Understanding how these outcomes relate to measurable attributes—such as genre, runtime, and release timing—can provide insight into how audiences respond to different types of films.

Modern media platforms generate large-scale public signals of audience engagement. The Movie Database (TMDB) provides metadata and engagement indicators such as popularity scores, vote counts, and average ratings. These indicators can be combined with other descriptive features (budgets, revenues, and categorical genre labels) to explore patterns across a wide set of movies. This project will use TMDB data to identify trends, compare groups of films, and later apply unsupervised and supervised learning methods to evaluate which features are most associated with different definitions of success.

Questions to Answer

  1. How are popularity and ratings distributed across the sampled movies?
  2. Do higher-rated movies tend to be more popular, or are these outcomes independent?
  3. Which genres appear most often in the dataset, and how do they differ in popularity and ratings?
  4. Does runtime relate to ratings or popularity?
  5. Are there clear differences in success metrics by release month or year?
  6. How do vote counts relate to rating stability (e.g., high votes vs low votes)?
  7. Do higher-budget films show higher popularity or revenue signals in the sample?
  8. Are revenue values highly skewed, and do extreme outliers dominate summary statistics?
  9. Can “top 25% popularity” be predicted from available features later in the course?
  10. Do genre combinations (e.g., Action + Adventure) appear with consistent success outcomes?
