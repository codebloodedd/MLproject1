What makes a movie “successful”?

This project explores how movie attributes (genre, runtime, budget, revenue, release timing, and audience engagement) relate to two common notions of success: popularity and ratings.

Topic Background

Movies are more than entertainment; they are cultural products influenced by creative decisions, marketing, distribution strategy, and audience preferences. A film’s “success” can be measured in multiple ways. Some films become widely known and discussed (high popularity), while others earn strong audience approval (high ratings).

This project uses movie metadata from The Movie Database (TMDB) to explore patterns linking movie attributes such as genre, runtime, budget, revenue, and release timing to measures of audience engagement including popularity scores and ratings.

Questions to Answer

  1. How are popularity and ratings distributed across the sampled movies?
  2. Do higher-rated movies tend to be more popular?
  3. Which genres appear most often in the dataset?
  4. Does runtime relate to ratings or popularity?
  5. Are there differences in success metrics by release timing?
  6. How do vote counts relate to rating stability?
  7. Do higher-budget films show stronger popularity or revenue signals?
  8. Are revenue values highly skewed?
  9. Can the top-25% popularity label be predicted later using supervised models?
  10. Do certain genre combinations appear consistently with higher success?
